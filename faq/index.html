How does the rebate work?

When you buy a new home, the builder typically pays a commission to your buyer's broker — usually 2–3% of the purchase price. With a traditional agent, that money stays with the agent. With keep, we give back up to 2/3 of it, credited directly to you at closing.

keep retains the greater of $8,000 or 1/3 of the commission. You receive the rest. The calculator on our site gives you a good estimate — your exact rebate is confirmed in the purchase contract and closing documents.

Why do I need to contact keep before my first visit?

This is the most important thing to understand about how keep works.

Builders require that your broker be registered with the community before your first visit. If you walk into a model home or sales office without keep already registered as your broker, the builder will not recognize us as your representative — and you lose your rebate.

One call before you visit is all it takes. We handle the registration from there.
What if the community requires in-person broker registration?

Some builders require an agent to physically register in person rather than by phone or online. If your community has this requirement, call or text us at least two business days before your planned visit. We will do everything we can to accommodate the request.

In rare cases where we cannot meet a builder's in-person requirement, we'll let you know and you can decide how to proceed.

How does keep work — do agents come to tours or meetings?

keep operates fully from our Arizona office. Our agents do not join you on home tours or visits to model homes — that's part of how we're able to offer the rebate we do.

What we do handle: registering you with the community, guiding you through the contract and upgrade selections, negotiating on your behalf, and supporting you through closing. For buyers who want to walk through homes on their own schedule without an agent in tow, this model works well.

Is the rebate guaranteed?

The rebate depends on a few things outside our control:

  • The builder must offer a commission to the buyer's broker at closing — most builders do, and this is standard practice in Phoenix new construction
  • Your lender must permit the credit (most do, but some loan types have restrictions)
  • The rebate cannot exceed lender credit limits, which may interact with builder incentives you're already receiving

We'll flag any of these issues early and help you maximize what's available. All estimates are subject to change until confirmed in the closing documents.

Does my lender need to approve the rebate?

Yes — rebates are processed as a credit through escrow at closing, which means your lender needs to account for it. Most conventional lenders allow this without issue. Some loan types (VA loans, for example) have specific rules.

If you're unsure, ask your lender early — we're happy to explain how the rebate works so they can confirm it's compatible with your financing. Call us if you have questions.

What does keep actually do for me?

As experts in new construction — having developed communities, built and run builder sales programs, and represented hundreds of Phoenix new home buyers — we handle everything a traditional buyer's agent handles, minus showing up to tours:

  • Register you with the community as your broker of record
  • Guide you through contract terms, upgrade selections, and builder negotiations
  • Review your purchase agreement and flag anything unusual
  • Coordinate with escrow and your lender through closing
  • Ensure your rebate is applied correctly at closing

We don't conduct home inspections or provide legal or financial advice — but we can recommend trusted inspectors, mortgage providers, and other professionals as needed.

Is this only for new construction?

Yes. keep works exclusively with buyers purchasing newly built homes sold directly by a licensed builder — homes that have never been occupied. We do not work with resale homes, for-sale-by-owner properties, or spec homes being sold by a third party.

Who is keep?

keep is a program offered by Conflux Real Estate, LLC, a licensed Arizona real estate brokerage. Our team has developed new home communities, built and run builder sales programs, and represented Phoenix new home buyers through hundreds of transactions. New construction is all we do.

Is there anything I need to sign?

Yes — before we register you with any community, you'll sign two short documents:

  • A Buyer-Broker Agreement, as required by Arizona law following the 2024 NAR settlement. This gives keep permission to act as your buyer's broker for the purpose of registration and representation — it does not make us your broker of record for any specific transaction. You remain free to use a different broker if you choose.
  • A keep Program Addendum, which outlines the rebate structure, scope of services, and what to expect from our representation model.

Both documents are straightforward and we'll walk you through them on your first call. Neither is binding — you are not obligated to purchase a home or continue working with keep at any point.
