Registration

Why you need to contact your broker before visiting a new home community

It's the one rule most Phoenix new home buyers don't know — and it costs them thousands.

The rule nobody tells you

When you walk into a new home community for the first time, the builder's sales team will ask one question: "Have you been working with a real estate agent?"

If you say no — or if you haven't registered a broker before that visit — you've just given up your right to have an independent buyer's broker represent you. And with it, any rebate that broker could have given you at closing.

This isn't fine print. It's standard practice across every major new home builder in Phoenix. Once you walk through that door unregistered, it cannot be undone.

Why builders care about this

Builders set up their sales processes to capture unrepresented buyers. If a buyer walks in without a broker, the builder's sales agent becomes the de facto point of contact — and the builder keeps the commission that would otherwise go to a buyer's broker.

This is legal and routine. The builder's agents are good at their jobs. But their job is to serve the builder's interests, not yours.

What "registration" actually means

When keep registers you with a community, we formally notify the builder that we are your buyer's broker of record. This is usually done by phone or online — it takes a few minutes.

From that point on, the builder recognizes keep as your representative. When you sign a purchase contract, keep is identified as your broker, and the commission is paid to us at closing — a portion of which we give back to you.

The key: Registration must happen before your first visit. Not the day after. Not when you decide you're serious. Before.

What to do

Before you visit any new home community in Phoenix:

  1. Call or text keep at 602-935-6585 — we'll ask you a few questions about the community and register you
  2. Get confirmation — we'll confirm registration and explain what to expect on your visit
  3. Visit the community — tour on your own schedule, without us in tow

That's it. One call, a few minutes, and your rebate is protected.

What if the community requires in-person registration?

Some builders require that a broker physically register in person rather than by phone or online. If your community has this requirement, contact keep at least two business days before your planned visit. We'll arrange everything.

Not sure if in-person registration is required? Call us and ask — we track registration policies for every active community in Phoenix.
